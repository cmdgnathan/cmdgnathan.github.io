Current work

I work at Zoox, where I lead efforts in collision avoidance, especially around evaluating and improving our low-latency, high-recall driving policy around critical and rare events. My background spans foundation model training, ML, computer vision, large-scale data, and agentic workflows.

Experience: Manipulation and Perception

Before Zoox, I worked on Manipulation and Perception for agricultural robotics with Bowery and Traptic (acquired by Bowery). I developed end-to-end vision models and 6dof arm controllers for dexterous strawberry harvesting — bridging the sim-to-real gap by prototyping in simulation and validating on deployed hardware.

PhD: Collaborative AI

I received my Ph.D. in Robotics from Georgia Tech, where I had the privilege of working with Prof. Zsolt Kira. My research is a core contributor to the domain of collaborative perception — multi-agent visual systems that learn what, when, and how to communicate.
